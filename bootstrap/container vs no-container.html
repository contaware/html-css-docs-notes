Container vs no container

The bootstrap container has left and right paddings of 12px which compensate with the row's left and right margins of -12px. The columns have left and right paddings of 12px which permit nesting rows in it.

If a none-bootstrap container is used, then the row is dragged out of the viewport with its negative margins. This will have no real effect on the left side but will cause a scroll on the right side. You can avoid showing the scrollbar by providing the overflow-hidden class to the none-bootstrap container.
