The following is for when you want a full-width content in a site which does not span the full-width.

vw measures also the vertical scrollbar, so that the full-width content will always overflow a few pixels. That's the reason why body has overflow-x: hidden. Those missing pixels are usually not a problem for images, while for content, it's important to have a left and a right padding.

For a cleaner solution, we must wait until the browser makers realize that the scrollbars should not take space, just pop-up when the user wants to scroll. Some few browsers used to support overflow: overlay, unfortunately that's now deprecated in favor of scrollbar-gutter: stable which reserves scrollbar space when the scrollbar is not shown. But as we do not know the size of the scrollbars for all browsers, scrollbar-gutter: stable is not helping.
